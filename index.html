Welcome to the Scientific Computing and Propulsion Laboratory at the University of Notre Dame. We will be located in McCourtney Hall as part of the Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering Department (pictured in the background). Our work focuses on developing numerical models of complex fluid flows, with particular emphasis on high-speed reacting flows and combustion. At the core of our research is model development rooted in artificial intelligence (AI), scientific machine learning (SciML), and computational fluid dynamics (CFD). Across these areas, we rely extensively on high-performance computing and place special emphasis on designing algorithms that exploit extreme scales in both data and computation. Our goal is to harness AI-enabled modeling advances to push the boundaries of design and deepen the physical understanding of next-generation power and propulsion systems across a wide range of devices, including detonation engines, scramjets, and gas turbines. Contact us at sbarwey@nd.edu for more information or if you are interested in joining the group. See more at the links below.

SCPL has a number of projects for which we are actively recruiting PhD students, with a start date for the Fall 2026 term. Project topics include scientific machine learning for high-speed reacting flows, in-situ CFD+ML modeling, and AI-based CFD workflow/algorithm design. Prospective students should have at least a Bachelor’s degree in Aerospace, Mechanical, or Chemical Engineering (or similar), with preferred candidates having a Master’s degree in these disciplines.

If you are interested, send a CV to sbarwey@nd.edu with any additional info you think would be relevant.
